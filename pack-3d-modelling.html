3D Modelling, Animation & Video Editing

Goal (≈10 hours)

Discover the fundamentals of 3D content creation: from modelling a simple object to animating it and presenting your work in a polished video. You’ll gain an appreciation for the pipeline from concept to final edit.

Suggested tools:

  • Blender or Tinkercad
  • Basic texture/material libraries
  • Video editor (OpenShot, DaVinci Resolve, iMovie)
  • Sound library for background music
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Model a household object. Choose a familiar item (e.g., mug, chair, lamp) and model it in 3D using Blender or another tool. Start with simple shapes, refine the mesh topology and apply basic materials. Document your modelling process with screenshots. Tip: Use reference images and incrementally add detail—avoid unnecessary polygons.
  2. Add animation. Animate your object using keyframes to create a short motion (e.g., rotate, bounce, open/close). Experiment with timing and easing to make the motion believable. Watch your animation from multiple angles to check for issues. Tip: Use the graph editor to tweak keyframe interpolation for smoother motion.
  3. Edit a showcase video. Capture renders of your model and animation, then assemble them into a 30‑second video using a video editor. Add a title card, background music and smooth transitions. Adjust the timing to match the rhythm of the music. Tip: Keep clips short and vary camera shots to maintain viewer interest.
  4. Reflect on the workflow. Write a brief reflection on what you learned about 3D modelling, animation and video editing. Discuss challenges you faced (e.g., UV mapping, lighting, rendering times) and how you overcame them. Suggest improvements for your next project. Tip: Use ChatGPT to help articulate technical hurdles and potential solutions.

Tip: Use available tutorials, sample projects or ask ChatGPT for guidance as you complete each task.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Your 3D model file (e.g., .blend or .obj)
  • A rendered animation clip
  • The final edited video with title and music
  • A reflection document

Send your files to Finlay@experience4grads.co.uk. Expect a response within 48 hours. If your work demonstrates competence, you’ll receive a certificate and a LinkedIn endorsement.
