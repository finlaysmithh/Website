Legal Research & Case Analysis

This pack introduces you to the fundamentals of legal research and case analysis. You will learn how to identify relevant cases, interpret legislation, and succinctly summarise complex legal materials.

Goal (≈10 hours)

Within about ten hours, you will develop research strategies, hone your case analysis skills and practise drafting professional legal documents. By the end of this pack, you should feel confident navigating law reports and statutes.

Suggested tools

  • Legal research databases (Westlaw, LexisNexis or open law repositories)
  • Statute books and consolidated legislation
  • Citation guides (OSCALA/Bluebook)
  • Note‑taking and outlining software
  • Case brief and memo templates

Guided tasks

  1. Case law research: Select a recent case in a practice area that interests you. Use an online legal database to research the case. Summarise the facts, issues, decision and ratio decidendi in your own words.
  2. Legislative analysis: Choose a statute relevant to the case you researched. Identify the key sections that apply and explain how the court interpreted them.
  3. Case brief preparation: Prepare a formal case brief using a standard format (Facts, Issue, Holding, Reasoning and Commentary). Include citations and pin‑point references.
  4. Comparative case matrix: Create a matrix comparing at least three cases on a similar issue. Highlight common legal principles and note any conflicting judgments.
  5. Legal memo: Draft a short memo advising a hypothetical client based on your research. Summarise the relevant law, apply it to the client’s scenario and conclude with your recommendation.

Tip: Allocate time slots across several days to complete each task. Reviewing your notes after a break will help you identify key principles and refine your analysis.

Need help getting started? Break the research into stages, use headnotes or summaries to orient yourself quickly and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar legal terms. Practise iteratively and document your progress.

What to submit

  • Your case summary notes
  • An analysis of the relevant legislative sections
  • A completed case brief
  • A comparative case matrix
  • A legal memo with your recommendation
