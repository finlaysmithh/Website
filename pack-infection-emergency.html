Infection Control & Emergency Care

Goal (≈10 hours)

Understand best practices for preventing infection and develop comprehensive emergency care resources. You’ll practise creating educational materials and checklists to improve preparedness in healthcare settings.

Suggested tools:

  • WHO or NHS guidelines
  • Canva, PowerPoint or drawing tools
  • Word processor or spreadsheet
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Research infection‑control practices. Examine guidelines for hand hygiene, personal protective equipment (PPE), sterilisation and waste disposal from reputable sources (e.g., WHO, CDC, NHS). Summarise at least five key practices, explaining why each is important and citing your sources. Tip: Create a short quiz for yourself to test retention of the guidelines.
  2. Create an educational poster. Design a poster or infographic that illustrates proper infection‑control procedures. Focus on clear visuals and concise text—use icons or diagrams to depict steps such as washing hands, donning PPE and disposing of sharps. Save your design as a PDF or image. Tip: Canva or Google Slides have templates that can help you get started.
  3. Draft an emergency care checklist. Develop a checklist for initial treatment of shock, bleeding, burns and fractures. For each condition, list key assessment points, interventions and when to seek additional help. Format your checklist so it can be easily followed during an emergency. Tip: Use bullet points and bold headings for rapid readability.
  4. Reflect on preparedness. Write a reflective piece discussing the challenges of maintaining infection control and emergency readiness in healthcare. Consider resource limitations, human factors and training needs. Suggest strategies teams can employ to improve preparedness. Tip: ChatGPT can help brainstorm barriers and solutions.

Tip: Use available tutorials, sample projects or ask ChatGPT for guidance as you complete each task.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Your summary of infection‑control practices
  • The educational poster or infographic file
  • Emergency care checklist document
  • A reflective essay on preparedness

Submit your materials to Finlay@experience4grads.co.uk. Reviews will be completed within 48 hours. If your work meets the standard, you’ll receive a certificate and a LinkedIn endorsement.
