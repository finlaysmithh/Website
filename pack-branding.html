Branding, Prototyping & Client Communication

Goal (≈10 hours)

Learn to develop a strong brand identity, prototype your ideas and communicate effectively with clients. These tasks blend strategic thinking with practical design and presentation skills.

Suggested tools:

  • Brand strategy worksheets
  • Figma, Adobe XD or Sketch
  • Presentation software (PowerPoint, Google Slides)
  • Online mood board tools (Pinterest, Miro)
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Define your brand. Select an organisation, cause or personal project. Write a brand brief that outlines its mission, values, target audience and market positioning. Create a mood board that reflects the brand personality, using images, colours and keywords. Explain how each element supports the brand identity. Tip: Research competitors to differentiate your brand.
  2. Develop visual guidelines. Design a logo, choose typefaces and define a colour palette. Produce a one‑page style guide demonstrating correct logo usage, typography hierarchy and colour combinations. Include examples of improper use to highlight what to avoid. Tip: Use a grid to align elements and maintain consistency.
  3. Prototype a product or service. Build a simple prototype (wireframe or clickable demo) of a product or service associated with your brand using a prototyping tool. Focus on core functionality and ensure visual consistency with your style guide. Include at least three user interactions. Tip: Start with low‑fidelity sketches to plan user flow before building the prototype.
  4. Simulate client communication. Draft an email, slide deck or video pitch presenting your brand identity and prototype to a client. Highlight the problem you solve, your design process and next steps. Include thoughtful questions to elicit feedback. Reflect on how you would adapt your communication for different clients (e.g., non‑technical versus technical stakeholders). Tip: Practice your pitch aloud to refine timing and tone.

Tip: Use available tutorials, sample projects or ask ChatGPT for guidance as you complete each task.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Your brand brief and mood board
  • The style guide (logo, fonts, colours)
  • A link or file of your prototype
  • Your client presentation materials with reflection
