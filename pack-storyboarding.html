Storyboarding, Logos & Motion Graphics

Goal (≈10 hours)

Learn to visualise stories, design memorable logos and animate them. You’ll practise storyboarding, logo creation and motion graphics to produce a cohesive short piece.

Suggested tools:

  • Paper & pencils or digital drawing apps
  • Vector software (Illustrator, Inkscape)
  • Motion graphics tools (After Effects, Blender, Canva)
  • Video editing software
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Draft a storyboard. Choose a concept for a 30‑second advertisement or explainer video. Sketch at least six frames that outline key scenes, camera angles and dialogue or captions. Use arrows to indicate movement and timing. Tip: Start with rough thumbnails to plan composition before refining your drawings.
  2. Create a logo for your story. Design a simple logo or wordmark that represents your brand or message. Experiment with different shapes, typefaces and colour schemes. Produce both colour and monochrome versions. Provide a brief rationale behind your choices. Tip: Use the golden ratio or grid systems to balance your design.
  3. Animate your logo. Import your logo into a motion graphics tool and animate it with a basic technique (e.g., fade in, scale up, reveal with a mask). Pay attention to timing and easing for smooth motion. Export your animation as a short video clip. Tip: Use keyframe curves in After Effects or Blender’s graph editor to fine‑tune easing.
  4. Reflect on your narrative. Write a short paragraph explaining your concept, how the storyboard guides the viewer and what you learned about motion design. Identify one thing you would change to improve the story flow or animation. Tip: Ask a friend to watch your clip and note where they lose interest or become confused.

Tip: Use available tutorials, sample projects or ask ChatGPT for guidance as you complete each task.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Scans or photos of your storyboard frames
  • Your logo files and design rationale
  • The animated logo video clip
  • A narrative reflection
