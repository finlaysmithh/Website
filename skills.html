ATS Skills & CV Optimisation

Prepare your CV and applications to pass Applicant Tracking Systems and stand out in the job market. Our training packs teach you how to craft ATS‑friendly CVs, tailor your applications and highlight your skills effectively.

What is an Applicant Tracking System?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is specialised software that employers and recruiters use to screen incoming CVs. When you submit your application, the ATS collects and scans your CV, searching for specific keywords and qualifications that match the job requirements. Based on this analysis, it ranks and sorts candidates so hiring managers can review the most relevant applications first.

Why optimise your CV?

Most job applications never even make it to a human recruiter because the ATS performs the initial screening. Candidates may be rejected without a second thought. More than 70 % of résumés don’t make it to a hiring manager’s desk, so tailoring your CV with the right keywords and skills is essential.

Industry‑specific ATS Skills

Each sector prioritises different technical and soft skills. Use these lists as a starting point when customising your CV. Remember to only include skills you actually possess and to mirror keywords from the job description.

Finance & Accounting

  • Microsoft Excel and advanced spreadsheet modelling
  • SQL and Big Data analysis
  • Financial reporting and cost accounting
  • Tax preparation and planning
  • Financial modeling, risk and investment analysis
  • Compliance, audit coordination and portfolio management

These competencies span foundational tools like Excel and SQL, as well as financial reporting, cost accounting, tax preparation, financial modelling and risk management.

Information Technology

  • Programming languages (e.g., Python, Java, C#)
  • Web development: HTML, CSS and JavaScript
  • Frameworks such as React, Angular or Vue.js
  • Database and cloud technologies (SQL/NoSQL, AWS, Azure)
  • DevOps practices and containerization (CI/CD, Docker, Kubernetes)
  • Cybersecurity, data analysis and visualization

These skills cover programming languages, web development, frameworks, database and cloud technologies, DevOps practices and data analysis.

Healthcare

  • Mathematics and accurate record keeping
  • CPR, ACLS and basic life support
  • Infection control and emergency care
  • Medication administration and patient education
  • Specialisations: pediatrics, oncology, geriatrics or neonatal care
  • Team coordination, cultural competency and stress management

The healthcare list emphasises numerical accuracy, life‑support techniques, infection control, emergency care, medication administration and specialist areas.

Art & Design

  • Graphic design and Adobe Creative Suite (Illustrator, InDesign, Photoshop)
  • Web and app development (HTML, CSS, UX/UI)
  • Photo editing and typography
  • Storyboarding, logo creation and motion graphics
  • 3D modelling, animation and video editing
  • Branding, prototyping and client communication

The creative skills listed draw from graphic design, Adobe software, web/app development, typography, storyboarding, 3D modelling and motion graphics.
