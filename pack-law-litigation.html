Litigation & Dispute Resolution

This pack covers the fundamentals of court proceedings and alternative dispute resolution (ADR). You will practise drafting pleadings, preparing for hearings and exploring mediation and arbitration strategies.

Goal (≈10 hours)

Across approximately ten hours, you will move through the key stages of civil procedure, explore discovery tools and practise drafting essential court documents. You’ll also examine strategies for negotiation and alternative dispute resolution.

Suggested tools

  • Civil Procedure Rules (or equivalent court rules)
  • Court form templates (claims, defences, witness statements)
  • ADR frameworks and guides
  • Negotiation and mediation checklists
  • Legal writing resources

Guided tasks

  1. Draft a statement of claim: Write a statement of claim for a simple civil dispute, setting out the facts, cause of action and relief sought. Ensure proper formatting and clarity.
  2. Prepare interrogatories: Draft a set of interrogatories (formal questions) that you would serve on the opposing party to gather information relevant to your case.
  3. Settle or proceed: Outline the key considerations for settling a dispute before trial. Discuss the pros and cons of settlement versus proceeding to trial.
  4. ADR outline: Prepare a short guide explaining the differences between negotiation, mediation and arbitration. Identify scenarios where each method might be preferred.
  5. Closing argument: Draft a persuasive closing argument for a hypothetical case, summarising the evidence and legal principles in favour of your client.

Tip: Spread your drafting and analysis over several days to allow time to research relevant rules and revisit your arguments with a clear mind.

Need help getting started? Break each task into manageable parts, consult your local rules of procedure and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress.

What to submit

  • Your drafted statement of claim
  • A set of interrogatories
  • A brief settlement strategy memo
  • Your ADR comparison guide
  • A closing argument for a hypothetical case
