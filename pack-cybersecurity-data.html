Cybersecurity, Data Analysis & Visualisation

Goal (≈10 hours)

Deepen your understanding of cybersecurity fundamentals while developing the ability to explore and visualise data. This pack blends theory and practice to help you make informed, ethical decisions about data and security.

Suggested tools:

  • Python & Jupyter/Colab
  • Pandas & Matplotlib
  • Excel or Google Sheets
  • Security resources (OWASP, NIST)
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Learn core security principles. Research the CIA triad (confidentiality, integrity, availability) and core concepts such as encryption, authentication and access control. Study common vulnerabilities from the OWASP Top 10 (e.g., SQL injection, cross‑site scripting) and summarise at least three mitigation strategies. Tip: Create a simple threat model for a hypothetical web application to apply these principles.
  2. Explore and clean a dataset. Download a public dataset of your choice (e.g., from Kaggle). Use Python’s pandas or Excel to inspect the data, identify missing or invalid entries and compute summary statistics. Visualise distributions using histograms or boxplots. Tip: Use functions like dropna(), describe() and corr() to gain insights into the data.
  3. Create visualisations. Build at least three different chart types to tell a story: for example, bar charts to compare categories, line charts to show trends and scatter plots or heatmaps to display relationships. Label axes clearly and write a short caption for each chart explaining what it shows. Tip: Try matplotlib.pyplot for customisation and use Excel’s chart wizard if you’re more comfortable in spreadsheets.
  4. Synthesise insights & reflect on security. Write a brief report (1–2 pages) that combines your data findings with a discussion of security and privacy considerations. How would you store or share this dataset securely? What ethical issues might arise from analysing personal data? Propose best practices for balancing utility and privacy. Tip: ChatGPT can help you frame ethical arguments and identify relevant legislation such as GDPR.

Tip: Use available tutorials, sample projects or ask ChatGPT for guidance as you complete each task.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Your security summary and threat model
  • Data cleaning and analysis scripts or Excel files
  • Image files or screenshots of your visualisations with captions
  • A concise report synthesising insights and security reflections

Email your work to Finlay@experience4grads.co.uk. Your submission will be reviewed within 48 hours. If approved, you’ll receive a certificate and a LinkedIn endorsement.
