Public Speaking & Presentation Skills

Goal (≈10 hours)

Develop the confidence and structure to deliver compelling speeches and presentations. You will research best practices, design visuals, practise delivery and seek feedback on your performance.

Suggested tools:

  • Presentation software (PowerPoint, Google Slides, Keynote)
  • Video recording tools (Zoom, smartphone camera)
  • Speech timer or mobile stopwatch
  • Public speaking guides & TED Talks
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Study effective presentations. Watch at least two TED Talks or other professional presentations and note techniques such as storytelling, slide design and vocal delivery. Outline what makes them engaging. Then choose a topic you care about and draft a basic outline for a 5–10 minute talk. Tip: Ask ChatGPT to suggest a structure (opening, body, conclusion) for your topic.
  2. Create a slide deck. Design a visual aid using your chosen presentation software. Follow design principles: clear headings, minimal text, high‑quality images and consistent layout. Add speaker notes below each slide to keep your talk organised. Tip: Use free templates or seek design inspiration from online galleries.
  3. Rehearse and record yourself. Practise delivering your presentation aloud several times. Record one of your runs using video conferencing software or your phone. Watch the recording to analyse your body language, pacing and clarity. Tip: Make small adjustments—pausing for emphasis, varying tone—and re‑record if necessary.
  4. Collect feedback and iterate. Share your video with a friend, mentor or upload privately and ask ChatGPT for constructive feedback. Adjust your slides and script based on the comments received. Deliver your talk again and note improvements. Tip: Practise in front of a mirror or empty room to build comfort with your material.

Tip: Break your preparation into manageable sessions—research, design, practise, revise—and consult public speaking guides or ChatGPT whenever you feel stuck.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Your presentation outline and notes
  • A PDF or link to your slide deck
  • A link or file of your recorded presentation
  • A short reflection on what you learned and how you incorporated feedback
