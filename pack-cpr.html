CPR, ACLS & Basic Life Support

Goal (≈10 hours)

Acquire up‑to‑date knowledge of life‑saving protocols and practise responding confidently to cardiac emergencies. By the end, you’ll have a clear action plan and an understanding of how to maintain your skills.

Suggested tools:

  • Official CPR & ACLS manuals (e.g., AHA)
  • Training videos or online simulations
  • Manikin or substitute (e.g., cushion)
  • Flowchart or diagram tool
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Research current guidelines. Read the latest CPR and advanced cardiac life support recommendations from a recognised authority (AHA, Red Cross or similar). Summarise the critical steps for adults, children and infants, including compression depth, rate and ventilation ratios. Highlight any changes from previous guidelines. Tip: Create a comparison table to reinforce differences.
  2. Practise techniques. Watch instructional videos and, if possible, attend a local training session. Practise chest compressions and rescue breaths on a manikin or cushion, focusing on proper hand placement and rhythm. Write a brief reflection on how the practice felt, difficulties encountered and how you corrected them. Tip: Use a metronome or song with 100–120 beats per minute to keep pace.
  3. Design an emergency response plan. Create a flowchart outlining the steps to take in a workplace or community scenario where someone collapses. Include roles (caller, first responder, AED operator), communication steps, when to switch rescuers and what to do post‑resuscitation. Think about accessibility of AEDs and emergency numbers. Tip: Tools like Lucidchart or hand‑drawn diagrams work equally well.
  4. Plan for continual learning. Investigate certification requirements and renewal intervals for CPR and ACLS. Identify local courses or online platforms for re‑certification. Write a short plan describing how you will maintain and improve your skills over the next two years, including scheduled practice sessions and resources. Tip: Use ChatGPT to locate reputable training providers.

Tip: Use available tutorials, sample projects or ask ChatGPT for guidance as you complete each task.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Your summary of CPR/ACLS guidelines
  • A reflection on your hands‑on practice
  • The emergency response flowchart or plan
  • Your personal plan for ongoing certification and practice

Compile these materials into a document or archive and email it to Finlay@experience4grads.co.uk. Your submission will be reviewed within 48 hours. If approved, you’ll receive a certificate and a LinkedIn endorsement.
