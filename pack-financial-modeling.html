Financial Modelling, Risk & Investment Analysis

Goal (≈10 hours)

Build a DCF & comparables model and investment memo for a listed company.

Suggested tools:

  • Excel
  • Yahoo/Investing data
  • Calculator
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Collect price & fundamental data; build drivers. Choose a publicly traded company and gather historical price data and financial metrics (revenue, EBITDA, CAPEX, tax rate) from sources like Yahoo Finance. Build a spreadsheet to organise and clean the data. Forecast key drivers such as revenue growth, profit margins and capital expenditures. Tip: Use Excel functions like INDEX/MATCH and create named ranges for clarity.
  2. Construct a DCF model and sensitise your assumptions. Build a five‑year free cash flow model using your drivers. Calculate the weighted average cost of capital (WACC) and choose an appropriate terminal growth rate. Use NPV and IRR to derive enterprise value and implied share price. Create sensitivity tables to see how changes in WACC or growth affect your valuation. Tip: Excel’s Data Table feature works well for one‑ or two‑way sensitivity analyses.
  3. Build a comparables table. Identify 3–5 similar companies and collect their EV/EBITDA and P/E multiples. Compute the median multiples and apply them to your company’s metrics to derive an implied valuation. Tip: Screeners like Finviz can help you find peers; ChatGPT can explain how to calculate enterprise value.
  4. Draft a one‑page investment memo. Summarise your thesis, key assumptions, valuation range (DCF and comparables) and major risks/opportunities. Use charts or tables to illustrate your findings. Keep the memo concise and organised with bullet points.

Tip: Use available tutorials, sample projects or ask ChatGPT for guidance as you complete each task.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Your model workbook (DCF and comparables)
  • A one‑page investment memo summarising your analysis
  • Sensitivity tables and any charts you created
