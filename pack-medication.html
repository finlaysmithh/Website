Medication Administration & Patient Education

Goal (≈10 hours)

Develop a deep understanding of safe medication practices, perform accurate calculations and create educational materials that empower patients. This pack fosters both technical accuracy and empathetic communication.

Suggested tools:

  • Pharmacology reference texts
  • Excel or calculator
  • Design software (Canva, Word)
  • ChatGPT

Guided tasks

  1. Summarise the “five rights”. Research the five rights of medication administration—right patient, medication, dose, route and time—and explain why each is crucial. Include examples of real incidents where failing a right led to patient harm. Tip: Use bullet points to make your summary concise and memorable.
  2. Create a dosage and infusion calculator. Develop a table in Excel that solves at least five sample dosage problems, including oral and IV infusion rates. For each scenario, show your formula or calculation steps. Double‑check your answers using a separate calculator. Tip: Use cell references and functions like ROUND to ensure precision.
  3. Design a patient information leaflet. Choose a common medication (e.g., insulin, antibiotics or a blood pressure drug) and create a one‑page leaflet. Use plain language to describe its purpose, how to take it, possible side effects and what to do if a dose is missed. Use diagrams or icons where appropriate. Tip: Consider translations or accessibility features if your audience is diverse.
  4. Reflect on patient communication. Write a reflection on strategies for educating patients about their medications. Discuss barriers to understanding (e.g., health literacy, language, anxiety) and how you would adapt your approach for different patient populations. Suggest ways to verify patient comprehension (e.g., teach‑back method). Tip: ChatGPT can help you brainstorm communication scenarios.

Tip: Use available tutorials, sample projects or ask ChatGPT for guidance as you complete each task.

Need help getting started? Break each task down into small steps, explore free tutorials online and ask ChatGPT to explain unfamiliar concepts. Practise iteratively and document your progress as you go.

What to submit

  • Your summary of the five rights with incident examples
  • The dosage/infusion calculation table with formulas
  • The patient education leaflet (PDF or image)
  • A reflective essay on patient communication

Send your work to Finlay@experience4grads.co.uk. Your submission will be reviewed within 48 hours. If approved, you’ll receive a certificate and LinkedIn endorsement.
